## F. Dimension summaries

- **Backups/DR:** Aurora is the only well-backed-up store; **Neo4j (identity SoR) has literally zero backups** — the single worst DR exposure. No DR runbook or RPO/RTO anywhere; everything single-region/single-account; Iceberg rollback capped at 7 days with an untested 90-day S3-version fallback, complicated by the catalog living in Aurora (coordinated restore never designed or tested).
- **Runbooks:** go-live path excellent, day-2 thin; the newest prod-critical procedures are **untracked files on one laptop**, the canonical migration script **destructively drops every schema** while GO-LIVE claims migrations are automatic, and one runbook still prescribes retired dbt/StarRocks steps as current.
- **Cost/scaling:** real spend ~$850-990/mo (2× the $500 target), fully masked by credits with an inert budget; dominant fixed costs are the EKS 1.32 extended-support surcharge ($432/mo, $360 avoidable) and VPC endpoints ($140/mo). At 10×, the first bottleneck is the single-JVM `local[*]` Spark transform tier, then Aurora's 2-ACU cap and the 555MB Redis fronting an OOM-prone 3-worker Trino fleet; Kafka and ingest have ample headroom.
- **Privacy/deletion:** RTBF components are well-built but **the end-to-end path is not wired**: nothing produces the orchestrator's trigger event, the Bronze erasure seam throws NotImplementedYet with a caller-less Spark job, the 7-day raw-retention TTL is unscheduled, and zero erasures have ever run in prod.
