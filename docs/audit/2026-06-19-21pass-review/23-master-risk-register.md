# Master Risk Register (2026-06-19)

Principal-level audit of the Brain AI-Native Commerce OS monorepo. Every risk below is anchored to a real `file:line` opened during the audit. Severities reflect the adversarial verdict-corrected values, not the raw finding labels.

## Scoring model

- **Likelihood (H/M/L)** = probability the latent defect manifests in a production-relevant way given current code paths and CI coverage.
- **Impact (H/M/L)** = blast radius / severity if it does manifest.
- **Score** = Likelihood × Impact on a 1–9 lattice: H=3, M=2, L=1 (e.g. H×H=9, H×M=6, M×M=4, H×L=3, M×L=2, L×L=1). Ties broken by tenant blast radius then by whether CI silently fails to detect.
- **Tenant blast radius**: All-tenants = a single defect degrades every brand; Multi = several/most tenants; Single = intra-service, no cross-tenant runtime path; None = build/maintainability only.

## Headline verdict

- **Score 9 (Critical) — 4 risks**, all "code does not yet exist or the guard that exists does nothing": three missing/stub bounded contexts (Identity ARC-4, Billing/Recommendation/job-orchestration ARC-5, Argo scheduler ARC-6) and one inert CI fence (RS-1). These are the gate to platform revenue, decision-intelligence value, finalized revenue, and tenant-isolation enforcement respectively.
- **Score 6 (High) — 3 risks**: the Postgres-spool ADR-003 drift with an un-provisioned Multi-AZ mitigation (ARC-3), the unenforced metric-engine fence + out-of-band StarRocks read (ARC-2), and the dead/inconsistent phone-guard policy (DP-1).
- **The dominant theme is enforcement theater, not isolated bugs**: the two highest-confidence boundary controls (RS-1 `boundaries/element-types` and ARC-1 `no-restricted-imports`) both exit 0 on known-violating files, so the architecture's stated invariants are unguarded at CI. Three of the four Critical items are missing implementations behind module boundaries that compile cleanly and are called by nothing — they fail silently (404 / stale `finalized` count / zero recommendations), not loudly.

**Bottom line: 20 ranked risks — 4 Critical, 3 High, 8 Medium, 5 Low. No cross-tenant data leak is presently provable; the brand predicate is still applied on every audited read path. The exposure is (a) Phase-1b feature absence that blocks revenue and decisions, and (b) erosion of the CI backstops that are supposed to keep the brand-scoped isolation seam from regressing.**
